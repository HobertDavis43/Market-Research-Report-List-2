<p><p>Field-Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGAs) are versatile semiconductor devices that can be programmed to carry out various functions. The FPGA market is categorized into three main types: SRAM-based FPGAs, which offer high performance and flexibility but require a power source to retain configuration; Antifuse FPGAs, known for their low power consumption and permanent programming, making them suitable for critical applications; and Flash FPGAs, which provide a balance between performance and reprogramming capabilities, allowing for easier updates.</p></p>
