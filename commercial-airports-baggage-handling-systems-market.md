<p><p>Commercial airports utilize advanced baggage handling systems to efficiently manage luggage. The market is primarily divided into two types: barcode and RFID systems. Barcode systems involve scanning luggage tags that contain barcode information, allowing for tracking and identification during transit. In contrast, RFID (Radio-Frequency Identification) systems use radio waves to automatically identify and track bags via tags embedded with microchips, enhancing accuracy and speed of handling. Both systems aim to improve operational efficiency, reduce lost baggage incidents, and elevate passenger experience.</p></p>
