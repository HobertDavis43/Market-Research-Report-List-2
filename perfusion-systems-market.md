<p><ul><li>Bioreactor</li><li>Microfluidic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Perfusion systems are critical in bioprocessing and tissue engineering, primarily categorized into bioreactors and microfluidic devices. Bioreactors facilitate the growth of microorganisms or cells in a controlled environment, optimizing nutrient delivery and waste removal to enhance productivity. In contrast, microfluidic systems manipulate small volumes of fluids through channels, allowing precise control over cell interactions and biochemical reactions. Both types enable advanced research and development in pharmaceuticals, regenerative medicine, and personalized therapies, showcasing their vital role in modern biomedical applications.</p></p>
